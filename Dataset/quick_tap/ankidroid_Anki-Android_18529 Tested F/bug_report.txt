### What are the steps to reproduce this bug?

Pressing some buttons opens a window with animations. During this time, you can touch anywhere on the screen which leads to unexpected results.

This is just one way of reproducing this bug:
1. Tap and hold on a deck.
2. Using one finger tap on rename.
3. Using another finger quickly tap and hold on a deck.

https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/15305dad-bf70-4a7e-9780-f5505619cad7

(the menus stacked on top of each other, in this case I tapped on the same deck so it appears as if it cancelled the rename pop-up)

### Expected behaviour

The app should ignore all inputs during animations, except for cancelling if that's possible.

### (Optional) Anything else you want to share?

This also works with "Create subdeck", or "Edit Description", tapping on another deck which opens the reviewer, "Restore from backup", "Import", "Export", "Check database", "Check media", the search button (this combined with rename creates two blinking "|" at the same time), the sync button, the 3 dot button, the 3 dash button and the plus button for adding cards.

It's also possible but very difficult to press the "Create deck" and the "Add" buttons in just the right way to open the pop-up and the "Add" screen on top of it.

What's worse is that you can cause a **crash** by holding on a deck then tapping on another deck with just the right timing to close the deck search pop-up and open the create subdeck pop-up, then type a name and hit ok. The app closes and you get a toast "Anki has encountered a problem".

You can do similar stuff in other screens like the browser and the app settings.

**TL;DR:** you can get windows stacking over each other, you can get sent to a different screen or shown something you didn't expect, and you can even crash the app.
