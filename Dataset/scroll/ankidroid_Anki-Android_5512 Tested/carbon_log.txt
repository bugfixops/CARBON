Record after fix:
https://drive.google.com/file/d/1fqXiEqFZq-BLgvw90JlSUdEDZSBFV2Xi/view?usp=drive_link




* The issue was resolved because the SeekBar bounds were exposed by UIAutomator Viewer at Step 16 (from the logs):
* * #8 [TEXT_ONLY] SeekBar text="10.0" bounds=[76,961][1004,1008]Gemini calculated the necessary x-coordinate to set the SeekBar to 50% for a span of $x=[76, 1004]$ (width = 928px), assuming a 0–200% range:
* * $x = 76 + 0.241 \times 928 \approx 300$
* * Tapping a SeekBar at a specific x-coordinate directly sets its value in Android, meaning no swipe was required. The summary at Step 17 confirmed success:
* * #25 [TEXT_ONLY] TextView text="50%"The run ended with success — Gemini declared the bug reproduced ("text in the card list is now visibly larger").
* 

Key takeaway: The previous run failed not because a swipe was needed, but because UIAutomator Viewer wasn't active. With the SeekBar visible and its bounds exposed in this run, a simple tap_screen at the calculated position was sufficient. The swipe_region action was never needed.
